My Services

Information Technology

Tutors would conduct one-on-one or group sessions with students to tutor them various IT concepts, technologies, programming languages, or software applications. These sessions could be held virtually through Microsoft Teams.

Computer Application Technology

Tutors would tutor on how to use specific computer applications effectively. This include popular office productivity suites like Microsoft Office (Word, Excel, PowerPoint), as well as specialized software for tasks such as web design as well as computer theory.

CyberSecurity

Tutors would introduce students to fundamental concepts and principles of cybersecurity, such as confidentiality, integrity, availability, authentication, and non-repudiation. They would explain the importance of cybersecurity in today's interconnected world and its role in safeguarding sensitive information.

Networking

Tutors would introduce students to the basics of networking, including concepts such as the OSI (Open Systems Interconnection) model, TCP/IP protocol suite, network topologies, and network devices (routers, switches, hubs, etc.).

Computer Literacy

Tutors would teach students how to operate a computer, including tasks such as turning it on and off, using the keyboard and mouse, navigating the desktop environment (Windows, macOS, Linux), and managing files and folders.

Ethical Hacking

Tutors would provide an overview of ethical hacking, explaining its purpose, principles, and ethical considerations. They would emphasize the importance of obtaining proper authorization and conducting testing within legal and ethical boundaries. Students would be introduced to various tools and techniques used in ethical hacking, including network scanning tools.

Computer Software Fix

Fixing computer software issues can encompass a wide range of problems, from simple glitches to more complex errors.

Computer Hardware Fix

Fixing computer hardware issues involves troubleshooting and potentially repairing or replacing malfunctioning components.
